# 🧩 Module 02: Problem Solving

> "Good problem solvers are made, not born."

This module focused on the **hypothesis-led problem solving approach**, a structured methodology used to tackle both quantitative/analytical and qualitative/conceptual challenges.

## 🎯 Core Objectives
*   **Define** the problem using structured tools and templates.
*   **Break down and structure** the problem in a way that leads to a solution.
*   **Plan** the path toward finding a recommended solution.

## 🛠️ Key Concept Definitions
| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problem Solving** | The process of finding answers to difficult and complicated personal and professional issues. |
| **Hypothesis** | A "best guess" for the solution to a problem based on limited evidence; a starting point for future discussion. |
| **Stakeholders** | A person with an interest or concern in the topic. |
| **Iterative** | Always improving, usually of a design or product. |
| **Solution set** | All the possible solutions and ideas for the problem. |
| **Bias** | A tendency to feel a particular way about something or someone which can impact how you think or behave. |

## 🏗️ The Problem-Solving Process

### 1. Power of Structuring & Prioritization
Instead of jumping straight to analysis, the focus is on **breaking the problem into component parts** and **prioritizing** which parts to focus on. This ensures that resources are not wasted on an inquiry that opens more questions than it resolves.

**The SodaComp Issue Tree Example:**
*   **Core Question:** What opportunities exist for SodaComp to improve its European market share by 3% by end of year?
*   **Key Branches:** Enhancing channel strategy (restaurants/cafes, online stores) vs. developing new/existing products (healthier products, natural juices).
*   **Expert Recommendation:** Conduct market research (focus groups) and competitive market scans to develop a product that outperforms the market, such as a healthier orange soda.

### 2. Types of Work
*   **Analytical/Quantitative:** Problems requiring an answer that is purely analytical, such as determining monthly grocery spend.
*   **Conceptual/Qualitative:** Problems that are more conceptual in nature, such as how to bring up an important problem to a supervisor.
*   **Hybrid Scope:** Most real-world projects require solving both analytical and conceptual aspects of a single problem.
